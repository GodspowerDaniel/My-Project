A young monk once went to see his superior: ‘Father,’ he said, ‘I must leave the monastery because I do not have a vocation to be a monk’. When the older monk asked why, the younger monk replied: ‘In spite of daily resolutions to be good-tempered, chaste, and sober, I keep on sinning. So I feel I am not suited to the monastic life.’ The older monk looked at him with love and said: ‘Brother, the monastic life is this: I rise up and I fall down, I rise up and I fall down’ The young monk stayed and persevered. (From Finding Sanctuary by Abbot Christopher Jamieson)
